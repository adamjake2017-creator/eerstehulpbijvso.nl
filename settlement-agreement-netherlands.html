What is a settlement agreement?

In the Netherlands, employers often end an employment contract with a vaststellingsovereenkomst: a settlement agreement by mutual consent. It records your severance, your end date, and other terms such as garden leave, a reference and any non-compete clause. Because you both agree, no permission from the UWV (the Dutch benefits authority) or the court is needed.

Don't sign right away

You are never obliged to sign immediately, even if you are given a deadline. By law you have a reflection period of 14 days after signing, during which you can withdraw without giving any reason. If that period is not mentioned in the agreement, it becomes 21 days. So you are never locked in straight away, and pressure to sign quickly has no legal basis.

Protect your unemployment benefit (WW)

This is the most important point. To keep your right to the Dutch unemployment benefit (WW), the agreement must be worded correctly: on the employer's initiative, on neutral grounds, and with a correct end date that respects your notice period. A wrong wording can cost you your benefit entirely. We check exactly this before you sign.

Frequently asked questions

What is a vaststellingsovereenkomst (settlement agreement)?

A vaststellingsovereenkomst is a settlement or termination agreement under Dutch law by which an employer and employee end the employment by mutual consent. It sets the severance, the end date and other terms, so no permission from the UWV or the court is needed.

Should I sign a settlement agreement right away?

No. You are never obliged to sign immediately. By law you have a reflection period of 14 days after signing to withdraw without giving a reason, and 21 days if this period is not stated in the agreement. Always have it checked first.

Will I keep my unemployment benefit (WW) after a settlement agreement?

Only if the agreement is worded correctly: on the employer's initiative, on neutral grounds and with a correct end date. If the wording is wrong, the UWV can refuse your unemployment benefit. This is the most important thing to check before signing.

Do I have to pay for legal help?

Usually not. In most settlement agreements the employer pays a contribution towards your legal costs. We check this and negotiate it for you.
